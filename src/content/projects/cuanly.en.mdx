Many storefronts look dynamic but actually hardcode their content — changing a price or adding a product means editing code and redeploying. Cuanly was built on the opposite assumption: the store owner should be able to manage their entire storefront themselves, without touching code.

## What I built

A marketplace for digital premium accounts (AI tools, streaming, music, productivity) with two clearly separated sides:

- **Visitor storefront** — landing, database-driven product catalog, SEO blog, categories, customer dashboard
- **Owner CMS panel** — manage products, custom pages, and store settings; all stored in the database and shown live

On top of that sit 80+ free tools (business and finance calculators) and support pages for a B2B audience.

## Technical decisions

**A genuinely database-backed CMS.** Every owner change — price, description, a new page — is stored in PostgreSQL via Prisma and shown instantly to every visitor. Not per-browser configuration, not static files needing a redeploy.

**Role-separated authentication.** Owners and customers have different login paths and permissions, guarded with JWT (jose) and password hashing (bcrypt).

**SEO-scale content.** The catalog and blog structure is designed for many indexed pages — categories, articles, and products each with their own URL.

## Outcome

The marketplace runs with hundreds of generated pages, powered by a catalog and CMS fully managed by the owner from the database — a foundation that can be operated without developer involvement for day-to-day work.
